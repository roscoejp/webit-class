History
The concept of open source and free sharing existed long before the birth of the microchip and the Digital Revolution.

The ideas behind open source have existed almost since the birth of human civilization, not always in the same form though.

In early human history, stories and recipes were shared.

In the early 1900s, thanks to actions taken by Henry Ford, the Motor Vehicle Manufacturers Association was formed in order for all US auto makers to share patents and licenses, free of charge.

It wasn't until the early 1980s, however, that the Free Software movement would be founded.

And almost a decade later... the Open Source movement.
